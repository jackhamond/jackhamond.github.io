## The Best Recordings

* Beethoven's symphonies - Herbert von Karajan (mid 60s or 70s recordings)
* Bach's piano pieces - Glenn Gould
* Bach's violin pieces - Fritz Kriesler
* Bach's B Minor Mass and Passion of St. Matthew - Sir John Eliot Gardiner
* Handel's Messiah - Sir John Eliot Gardiner
* Pretty much all orchestral baroque - Sir John Eliot Gardiner
* Wagner's The Ring Cycle - Sir John Sulty
* In general, Wagner's operas - Herbert von Karajan

### Don't want

* Arturo Toscanini for Verdi

## General Notes:

* Who was the best in regards to pure talent? It is most definitely Mozart. He did not have to try. He would write on the back of a napkin. He is like Shakespeare with poetry who it is said never blotted a line.
* Who was the overall best in regards to knowledge, pure talent, technical ability, etc.? It is Bach.
* Bach wrote 1008 pieces, Mozart 626.
* Make sure to listen to The Ring Cycle with libretto. Wagner was his own librettists unlike everyone else
